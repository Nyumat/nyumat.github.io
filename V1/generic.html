My Goal

By now we've all heard the cliche developer 'dream'. Being 25, working at a FAANG company in the Bay Area, all while making "only" 400k Total Comp. While the perks, glam, and benefits may be nice, Different people have various needs and wants. For me, doing cutting edge, real world problems provides the most satisfaction. My 'dream'....from making A.I. that can not only learn tendencies you have on your computer, even to technology to use this data and build a production plan for you, but also writing algorithms, such as for a Map Pathfinder Algortihm to track the shortest distance to each hiking trail within a certain radius. Ideas like these are IMO, what developers live off of.

I am fortunate to be raised in to a time where, practically everyone these days has access to all the information they'd ever need...at their fingertips. I personally find it striking that anyone, if they so may please, can develop the next "SnapChat" or "Google" on their own. Also, having all these fantastic free resources and professionals that are always willing to help. Now couldn't be a better time to try and innovate and solve complex problems.

For example, with the current state of the justice movements, an app/software can be developed that takes all of the BLM Lobbying/Petition/Donation websites and puts it all into one place, so that people who want to seek justice will have everything in one place. (This idea may or may not be in development right now :p)...but even another example, such as creating a simple program that can automate the process of my friends and I searching for places to skate at. I currently have the complex where I want to skate, but all the parks/spots are hard to hear about or even see, due to them all being unmarked on Google Maps. By making a social media like app where skaters can go on to to enter new spots they find and explore spots that have never been seen, it'll sure make life a hell of a lot easier.

Quite simply, my goal with this entire engineering part of my life is to one day build and maintain scaleable, clean software & or hardware that millions who need it across the globe have access too. I want to be a Foster for Inclusivity, promote diversity within STEM, and change lives in the process. No one said it was going to be easy, because let's be real, even if it was, a wise man once said, everyone would be doing it then.
